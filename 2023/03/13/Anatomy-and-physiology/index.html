Artificial Hip Joint and Total Hip Replacement

NAME:Chenyu Gu
SID:12011123
Abstract:
The brief introduction to Artificial Hip Joint and the technique of Total Hip Replacement. First describe four disease which is related to hip joint and then give a overview of the fundamental functions and structures of the hip joint from anatomy perspective and after that we describe the specific solution of hip replacement and the structure of the artificial hip joint. Finally, we points out the limitation and the drawback of the artificial hip joint and some overview of the futher development.
Keywords:
Anatomy, Physiology, Hip joint, Total Hip Replacement

The Anatomy Principle of Hip Joint

Primary disease of the hip joint

The hip joint plays an important role in supporting the human body and providing movability. While there are four primary diseases of the hip joint that may cause damage to the hip joint and result in a total or partial hip replacement. And in this article, we will mainly focus on the first two diseases.

Osteoarthritis

It is shown that osteoarthritis is one of the most common joint diseases in the world and the previous study shows that in the western countries, it has been over 80 percent of people aged over 75 years suffer from osteoarthritis and it is a common, degenerative disease.

The mechanism of osteoarthritis is that the cartilage which is between the femoral head with the acetabulum wears each other and it will cause the abrasion of the two connective cartilage tissues and eventually will reduce the buffer effect of the cartilage and causing the two bones to scrape against each other such as between raw bone and raw bone. If this situation happens the bones which scrape each other will become pitted, eroded, and uneven.

Booth arthritis has different joint pathology, including bureau’s stove damage and joint cartilage loss, abnormal reshaping and wear of joint bone bones, osteophytes (bone growth on the edge of the joints), ligament relaxation, and weakening of joint muscles around the joints. It also includes slid diaphragm dilation and inflammation.

And the symptoms of osteoarthritis often occur with the primary symptoms including pain in the joint, stiffness, and instability. Besides in some cases, the motion of the leg can be greatly restricted. One of the most common symptoms is the pain in the hip or groin area when the patient is doing some weight lifting activity, and for these patients to compensate these symptoms always show limping to reduce the force on the arthritic hip.

And the most common diagnostic criteria include joint pain, x-rays of femoral and acetabular osteophytes from previous months, and x-rays of narrow space in the hip joint. And patients who got osteoarthritis often have large bone spurs around the joint which these tissues will limit the motion.

The cause of this disease is quite simple. Because it is a degenerative disease when the age goes up the wear and tear of the hip cartilage and by the age of 70–89 years, the incidence of symptomatic knee OA increases rapidly. And also due to the previous fractures that occurred or the childhood disorders which may lead to a degenerative hip, the incidence of osteoarthritis also shows an increase.

The osteoarthritis has several risk factors including age and gender, obesity, bone density and osteoporosis, sex hormones, local mechanical risk factors, and muscle strength and weakness.

Rheumatoid Arthritis

Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune disease, a chronic inflammatory disease, which can cause joint pain, stiffness and swelling.

The rheumatoid arthritis has different factors among different genotypes and different environmenal backgrounds.This may lead to hypoerythrocytes, lung inflammation, and heart inflammation. It may also have fever and lack of vitality. The usual symptoms are gradually exceeding weeks to months.

While the cause of this disease is still not clear, but it is related to genetic and environmental factors. The mechanism of action includes the body’s immune system attacking joints, causing inflammation and thickening joint capsule, which usually affects bones and cartilage. In some studies, researchers believe that viruses or bacteria may cause diseases with genetic tendencies with rheumatoid arthritis. And many doctors think this is one’s immune system that attacks its own synovial tissue of the joint. The symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis are quite similar to the osteoarthritis which both result in joint pain, loss of motion, and swelling while rheumatoid arthritis has some other symptoms like loss of appetite, fever, energy loss, anemia, and rheumatoid nodules.

While the diagnosis of this this disease is quite clinical which includes patients’ pain, stiffness, and swelling of multiple joints in a bilateral, symmetric pattern. And the emergence of this disease is quite random which may last for over weeks-months combined with fatigue and tiredness.

Developmental Dysplasia of the Hip

This type of disease often occurs in the infant baby and is a lifelong disease. The probability of everyone is one thousandth, and the diagnosis of dysplasia of hip joints is usually detected in the first year of the baby. Symptoms include a reduced leg movement on the affected buttocks, shortening the affected legs, or asymmetric leg position.

Avascular Necrosis

When the blood circulation is not enough and it may cause a lack of nutrients in the bone and the bone will be starving and resulting in avascular necrosis. And when the starving bone cells die the bone structure collapse and resulting in the collapse of the hip joint. This type of disease often happens in younger and middle-aged adults.

And the cause of this disease is often the blockage of the blood circulation to the bone tissue.

Also, the symptoms are the pain in the hip and stiffness and the femoral head may weaken and collapse.

The affected tissues

So in this article, we will focus on the first two diseases that is the Osteoarthritis and Rheumatoid Arthritis.

In these two diseases, Osteoarthritis is a degenerative disease that affects the cartilage tissue in the hip joint and the Rheumatoid Arthritis is an autoimmune disease that affects the synovial tissue of the joint. And here we describe the tissue functions of the two different tissues.

Cartilage tissue

Cartilage tissue is a type of connective tissue which have abundant matrix and collagen and it is a smooth elastic tissue. (Figure shown below)

Section from mouse joint showing cartilage (purple)

The basic components of cartilage tissue are the chondrocytes which serve as the basic cell in the cartilage tissue and produce a large number of the collagenous extracellular matrix. And besides is a vast number of the ground matrix and the ground matrix, it contains many proteoglycans and elastin fiber.

The basic function of cartilage tissue can be divided into three parts according to its mechanical property, frictional property, and repair property.

Because of its elasticity, it can cover the end of the bones in the joint. It can absorb the assault and provides a buffer in two bones and avoid tear and wear. Also due to its rigidity, it can serve the purpose to make sure the tube is open such as the carina and the cricoid cartilage.

And also in the cartilage tissues, it contains abundant of lubricin which is a glycoprotein in the cartilage. This type of protein serves as the friction and can provide wear protection of cartilage.

While cartilage has a weak ability to heal due to the lack of blood supply so that the decomposition of the new matrix is very slow so cartilage damage is very hard to heal.

Synovial tissue

Synovial tissue is also a type of connective tissue and it basically contains loose vascular connective tissues. These tissues can make up the membranes surrounding the joints and the sheaths to protect tendons.

Synovial joint

The function of synovial tissue can provide a plane of disconnection between the outside solid ligaments and the inner articular cartilages and make sure that this joint can move. It also provides a changeable shape that can make sure the movement of the joint. And it can control the medium fluid in the cavity so that to allow the molecular transfer.

And synovial tissue is quite simple but its function is highly precise and it is the target of many autoimmune diseases.

The normal physiology of hip joint

The hip joint area is located on the side and front of the hip area, lower than the iliac cricket, and covered with a large rotor of the femur.

Bones of the hip region

The hip region contains a lot of tissues including bone structures, joint space, and soft tissues like muscle tendons, ligaments, muscle, and neurovascular structures. And also the hip joint contains the fusion of three big joints of the pelvis which forms the hip bone. The hip joint contains different types of tissues including the bone tissue, the connective tissue like the cartilages, and the muscle tissue including tendons, muscle fibers, and so on.

And the function of the hip joint has different situations. In static situations and dynamic situations, the hip joint is to support a human’s body weight. And also it plays an important role in maintaining the balance and maintaining the pelvic tilt. And the hip joint shows that it is the most rigid ball-and-socket joint and paly an important role in the range of motion.

The social burden of the diseases

In this part, we will basically describe the social burden of the first two diseases which is the Osteoarthritis and the Rheumatoid Arthritis.

The social burden of Osteoarthritis

It is estimated that osteoarthritis has a great prevalence in age, gender, and ethnicity. And an analytical statistic shows that almost 43% of people with OA are 65 or older and almost 88% of people with OA are 45 or older. Also, it shows that the most incidence age is between 55 years and 64 years old. Besides, women have more ratio and non-Hispanic whites also have a higher ratio.

And the cost of the osteoarthritis is calculated by adding the absenteeism costs and the average direct cost will sum up to the direct cost $65 billion and indirect cost $17 billion. It shows that it has become the third most rapidly rising condition associated with disability.

Also for the patients, they will suffer from the pain and people with the osteoarthritis will have both chronic pain and episodic pain and these pain will result in other mood disturbances. They will feel depressed and will feel they might be isolated by the society. The osteoarthritis will result in the walking disability and will affect the normal activity. Besides the activity limitations, the patient will feel very difficult to do stooping, bending and kneeling and they even cannot walk over 3 blocks and they cannnot handle with heavy objects.

The social burden of Rheumatoid Arthritis

For the rheumatoid arthritis, it is quite similar to the osteoarthritis disease.

According to a survey collected by almost 4200 patients who are suffered from the rheumatoid arthritis.

And to sum up, the patients have to suffer from the disease-modifying treatment and the joint pain and the economic burden of this disease is still very high in terms of the high cost of the modern drug treatment and the rehabilitation treatment.

The Engineering Mechanism of Artificial Hip Joint

After we describe the basic function of the hip joint and the disease related to the hip joint, a treatment to this type of disease is to use artificial hip joint to perform total hip replacement.

The goal of the artificial hip joint

To deal with the diseases we have talked about above and if hip pain has already affected daily activities and non-surgical treatment is ineffective or no longer effective, in this situation the hip joint replacement is an option.

The artificial hip joint is used in the hip joint replacement and it serves as a replacement for the original hip joint. To perform the ability to support the body weight whether in static movement or in dynamic movement, besides the hip joint disease will cause a lot of pain to the patient. The artificial hip joint is applied to ease or erase the pain caused by the disease.

Besides, the limitation caused by the disease like activity limitation and work limitation can be solved. The patients with artificial hip joints can do these tasks like the normal people without suffering from pain.

Also, the artificial hip can last for a long time, this can save a lot of money for the patients without paying for further maintenance and another replacement which benefits the global economy.

The major components of the artificial hip joint

The artificial hip joints usually contain two or more components which typically consist of a stem that is fitted into the femur named the femoral stem and a ball mounted on the top of the stem and the cup mounted on the acetabular provide a smooth sliding surface for the ball.

Artificial hip joint

To detailly describe the components that the damaged femoral head is removed and the metal stem (femoral stem) is used to replace and place it into the center of the patient’s femur.

The metal or ceramic ball is placed on the upper part of the femoral stem, which is used to replace the damaged femoral head.

Besides, the damaged cartilage surface of the socket which is the acetabulum is removed, then this will replace by the metal socket.

Between the new ball and the socket surface, a different material spacer is inserted to allow for a smooth gliding surface.

And it shows that there are different choice of the prosthesis which depends upon the individual and different surface modification has been performed. In the early 1960s, Sir John Charnley improved hip replacement by developing low-friction prostheses; this is still the standard by which new changes are measured.

Total Hip Replacement

There are three major types of hip replacement treatment which are total hip replacement which is the most common, partial hip replacement and the hip resurfacing.

While to treat the hip joint disease the most common type of replacement is total hip replacement (also called total hip arthroplasty).

Cemented total hip replacement

German surgeon Glück uses bone cement for better fixation, he uses these elements to fix the bone-cement and cement-implant interface for durable fixation, a highly dependent technique for cemented total hip arthroplasty , because the manufacture of bone-cement-implant composites is made during surgery.

And with years goes by, the cementation technique has changed largely, early method has limited in the preparation of the bone bed and will result in low penetration into the cancellous bone.

Therefore, there are generally two ideas for the design of the pool bumper: one is the conical -sliding or stress -closed design, and the other is the composite -beam or shape -closed design. The cone can easily optimize the load distribution of the surrounding bones, and the fixed height of the composite beam depends on the shape of the implant and the composite fixing of the handle and the cement and cement and the bone.

Uncemented total hip replacement

The failure to implement the cemented stems is quite frequent because of the bone destruction and resorption.

This happens because of the inflammatory response caused by the cement particles and researchers think that the total hip replacement need to be directed implanted without adding the cement and besides that removing the cement can effectively ease the polymethyl methacrylate debris caused by the cement disease.

Thus, to remove the cemented components and using cementless femoral and acetabular components to take the place of the cement and to provide the enough stability and tomato the bone bound to the implant.

Thus, research has designed to make the stem as rough as possible which could be with a porous coating to allow intimate bony apposition to anchor the implant. Once the biological biological is implanted and implanted in the bones, the femoral parts will allow the biometer to pass through the joints and cause normal function.

The critical design parameters

As is mentioned before the spacer which is used to insert into the ball and the socket to perform smooth gliding surface.

This can be plastic, ceramic or metal spacer for different performance.

Besides, the two methods of total hip replacement is also described above, one is cemented total hip replacement and the other is uncemented total hip replacement, the material and surface modification to perfect implant the stem is also another critical design parameter.

The Outlook of Artificial Hip Joint

The limitations and drawbacks

  1. An artificial hip isn’t as good as a natural hip.

When doing some extreme positions such as crouching, the displacement of the hip joint is not always optimal.

  1. Change in leg length.

The artificial hip joint may not have so much precise compared to the original hip joint and will result in the change in leg length.

  1. Aseptic loosening of cemented femoral prostheses after THA is a potential cause of pain and loss of function

The loosen when performing the cemented femoral component and it may result in another revision and this may because of the selection of the patients and the materials and designs of the implants

  1. Instability

Dislocation is a common complications after THA. Risk factors include neuromuscular and cognitive impairment, patient disobedience, and previous hip surgery. Recent improvements in the repair of posterior soft tissue after the initial THA indicate a decrease in the incidence of dislocation.

  1. Infection

As the bacteria became more and more resistive, the probability of being infected is increasingly complex. So the infection is another limitation.

  1. Limit life time

Potential methods

The cemented hip replacement still considered as the gold standard, while the cement implanted has potential of aseptic loosening. So some researches use hybrid THA that is using a press-fit acetabular component with a cemented femoral components and it may show better survival rate.

What kind of breakthrough can improve it

Optimization of bone-implant interface is still an important research area in this field. New fixed technologies include non-cement components, which depend on the growth of bones to porous or rough surfaces. Other surface modifications of the implant are designed to directly attach to the bones. THR surface adds several types of calcium phosphate ceramics [for example, hydroxylhstone (HA)].
